Understanding F1 Hybrid Power Units

What is a Hybrid Power Unit?

Formula 1 cars use hybrid power units (HPUs), a combination of a traditional internal combustion engine and advanced electric systems. The main engine provides raw power, while electric systems capture and store extra energy for strategic bursts of speed.

How the Energy Recovery System Works

The ERS has two main parts: the Motor Generator Unit-Kinetic (MGU-K) and the Motor Generator Unit-Heat (MGU-H). These capture braking and heat energy, storing it in a battery for later use. Drivers use this extra energy to overtake or defend positions.
